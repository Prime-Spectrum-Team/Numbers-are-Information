Every natural number encodes intrinsic structural information through its prime factorization. 60 = 2²×3×5 has SpectralAddress [2,1,1,0,0,0,0] — a coordinate in 7-dimensional prime space. This information is deterministic, compositional, and computable.

Complementary Viewpoint
From Ordinals to Coordinates
The standard view treats integers as ordinal positions. The complementary view developed here treats each number as a coordinate in prime space — its factorization encodes information that is intrinsic, deterministic, and compositional.

❌ Standard View

60 = sixty units
A number is an ordinal position. Its operationally relevant property is magnitude. To compare 60 and 18, you subtract: |60 − 18| = 42. This tells you the distance but nothing about their structural relationship.

✅ Complementary View

60 = [2,1,1,0,0,0,0]
A number is a coordinate in prime space. 60 = 2²×3×5 → SpectralAddress [2,1,1,0,0,0,0]. Its factorization determines what it resonates with (shared prime factors), what it's coprime to, and which arithmetic class it belongs to.

The Seven-Class Partition
Arithmetic Classification of Every Number
Every natural number belongs to exactly one of 7 arithmetic classes, based on two invariants: the total prime factor count Ω(n) and the residue class of primes modulo 6. This partition is exhaustive and forms a finite commutative monoid (S, ⊗) under induced multiplication.

Resonance Relation
Structural Co-Divisibility
Two numbers resonate when they share prime factors: R(i,j) = Ω(gcd(i,j)). This is computable in O(K) via component-wise minimum of their SpectralAddresses. Approximately 61% of integer pairs are non-resonant (coprime), matching the classical value 6/π².

49 Laws. Zero Exceptions.
